Exploring Space

Space is amazing

The universe is a mind-boggling expanse of space that contains galaxies, stars, planets, and countless celestial objects. It is estimated to be around 13.8 billion years old and continues to expand. Our understanding of the universe has grown through the study of astronomy and space exploration.

Explore Space
Guion Bluford
He made history as the first African American in space. His first mission was aboard the Challenger in 1983. Bluford went on to complete three more shuttle missions before retiring.
Buzz Aldrin
Buzz Aldrin walked on the moon during the Apollo 11 mission. Compared to his colleague, Aldrin went on more NASA missions and spent nearly 300 hours in space!
Ellison Onizuka
Ellison Onizuka was a successful U.S. Air Force test pilot before joining NASA in 1978. Just seven years later, he became the first Asian American in space. Unfortunately, he was killed during his second mission at age 36 as a crew member of the Challenger space shuttle.
Neil Armstrong
The most famous astronaut of all time, Neil Armstrong became the first man to walk on the moon in 1969. During this incredible Apollo 11 mission, he said these iconic words: “That’s one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind.
